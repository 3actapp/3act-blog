## The Neuroscience of Habit Formation: Why Apps Work

Before we dive into the apps themselves, it’s crucial to understand *why* they are so effective. Habit formation is a neurological process. As neuroscientist Dr. Andrew Huberman explains, our brains are wired to create and follow patterns to conserve energy. When we repeat an action consistently in the same context, the brain forms a strong neural pathway, a "habit loop" consisting of a cue, a routine, and a reward. [2]

A habit tracker app acts as a digital scaffold for this loop. It provides the **cue** (notifications and reminders), a platform to execute the **routine** (checking off the habit), and a sense of **reward** (streaks, points, and visual progress). This digital reinforcement helps to solidify the neural pathways of your desired habits, making them more automatic over time.

But there's a missing piece to this puzzle for many. The solitary journey of self-improvement can be a lonely one, and when motivation wanes, it's easy to fall off. This is where the power of social accountability comes in, a concept we explore in our article on [the science of social accountability](/posts/the-science-of-social-accountability/).

## Top 10 Habit Tracker Apps for iPhone

### 1. Streaks

**Best for:** The minimalist who loves a clean design and the motivating power of a simple, unbroken chain.

Streaks has long been a darling of the App Store, and for good reason. It won an Apple Design Award, and its interface is a masterclass in elegant simplicity. The app is built around a single, powerful concept: don't break the chain. You can track up to 24 habits, and the goal is to build the longest possible streak for each one.

Its integration with the Apple ecosystem is seamless. It syncs with Apple Health for automatic tracking of fitness and health-related habits, and its widgets are some of the most beautiful and functional you'll find. For a one-time fee, you get a powerful, aesthetically pleasing app that does one thing exceptionally well.

**Pros:**
- **Stunning, minimalist design:** A joy to use every day.
- **Excellent Apple Health integration:** Automates tracking for many habits.
- **One-time purchase:** No subscription fees to worry about.

**Cons:**
- **Limited to 24 habits:** May not be enough for power users.
- **Rigid streak-based system:** Breaking a long streak can be demotivating.
- **No social features:** A purely solitary experience.

### 2. Habitica

**Best for:** Gamers and anyone who needs a dose of fun and fantasy to stay motivated.

Habitica takes a radically different approach to habit tracking, transforming your life into a role-playing game (RPG). You create an avatar, and your real-life habits, dailies, and to-dos become monsters to conquer. Completing tasks earns you gold, experience points (XP), and items to level up your character. It's a brilliantly creative concept that can make the mundane act of habit tracking genuinely fun.

The social aspect is also a core part of the experience. You can form parties with friends to fight bosses together, and if one person fails to complete their daily tasks, the entire party takes damage. This adds a layer of social accountability that is missing from many other apps.

**Pros:**
- **Highly engaging and fun:** The gamification is incredibly well-executed and can be a powerful motivator.
- **Social features:** You can form parties with friends to fight bosses and keep each other accountable.
- **Customizable:** A vast array of items, pets, and mounts to collect.

**Cons:**
- **Can be complex:** The RPG elements can be overwhelming for some users.
- **Interface feels dated:** The design isn't as modern or clean as some other apps on this list.
- **Focus on fantasy, not reality:** The virtual rewards may not translate to real-world motivation for everyone.

### 3. Productive

**Best for:** The organizer who likes to schedule their habits into morning, afternoon, and evening routines.

Productive is a visually appealing and feature-rich app that excels at helping you organize your day. It allows you to schedule habits for specific times, which is perfect for creating structured routines like the one described in our guide on [how to build a morning routine you will actually stick to](/posts/how-to-build-a-morning-routine-you-will-actually-stick-to/). The app also offers a library of pre-built "challenges" and habit packs to help you get started, covering everything from mindfulness to fitness.

One of its most forgiving features is that habits aren't marked as "missed" until the day is over, which can reduce the anxiety associated with maintaining a perfect streak. While it offers a lot of features, the free version is quite limited, and the frequent prompts to upgrade can be intrusive.

**Pros:**
- **Excellent for routine planning:** The time-based scheduling is a powerful feature.
- **Great for beginners:** The curated habit packs provide a good starting point.
- **Flexible streak tracking:** Reduces the pressure of daily check-ins.

**Cons:**
- **iOS only:** No web or desktop version available.
- **Aggressive upselling:** The constant upgrade prompts can be annoying.
- **Limited analytics:** The data and insights are not as deep as other apps.

### 4. HabitShare

**Best for:** Those who want to share their habit-tracking journey with friends for mutual accountability.

HabitShare is built on the principle that you're more likely to stick with your habits if you share them with others. It's a social habit tracker at its core, allowing you to share your progress with friends and family. You can choose which habits to share and which to keep private, giving you complete control over your privacy. If you need help finding someone to share your goals with, check out our guide on [how to find an accountability partner](/posts/how-to-find-an-accountability-partner/).

The app's interface is straightforward and easy to use, focusing on the social aspect of habit tracking. You can comment on your friends' progress and send them messages of encouragement, creating a supportive community around your goals. It's a simple but effective tool for anyone who thrives on external accountability.

**Pros:**
- **Social accountability:** Share your progress with friends to stay motivated.
- **Privacy controls:** You decide which habits to share and which to keep private.
- **Simple and focused:** The app does one thing well without unnecessary features.

**Cons:**
- **Limited features:** Lacks the advanced analytics and customization options of other apps.
- **Dependent on your network:** The social features are only useful if you have friends on the app.
- **Basic design:** The interface is functional but not as polished as some competitors.

### 5. Done

**Best for:** Visual thinkers who appreciate a clean, colorful, and data-rich interface.

Done is a visually striking habit tracker that helps you track your progress with beautiful charts and graphs. It allows you to track both habits you want to build and habits you want to quit, and you can set daily, weekly, or monthly goals. The app's design is clean and intuitive, making it easy to see your progress at a glance.

One of Done's standout features is its ability to track habits multiple times a day. This is great for habits like drinking water or practicing mindfulness. The app also offers a variety of color themes and icons to personalize your experience. While the free version is limited to three habits, the premium version unlocks unlimited habits and advanced features.

**Pros:**
- **Beautiful and intuitive design:** The visual interface is a pleasure to use.
- **Flexible tracking:** Track habits multiple times a day, and set daily, weekly, or monthly goals.
- **Data visualization:** The charts and graphs provide a clear overview of your progress.

**Cons:**
- **Limited free version:** You can only track three habits for free.
- **No social features:** Lacks the accountability of a social network.
- **Subscription model:** Requires a subscription for unlimited habits and features.

### 6. Habitify

**Best for:** Data-driven users who want to organize their habits into specific areas of their life.

Habitify is a powerful and feature-rich habit tracker that allows you to organize your habits into different "areas," such as "Morning Routine," "Fitness," or "Work." This is a great way to keep your habits organized and focused. The app also provides detailed stats and charts to help you analyze your performance and identify areas for improvement.

Habitify also has a strong social component, allowing you to share your progress with friends and join a community of like-minded individuals. The app integrates with Apple Health and offers a dark mode for comfortable use at night. While the free version is generous, the premium version unlocks even more features, including unlimited habits and advanced analytics.

**Pros:**
- **Habit areas:** Organize your habits into different areas of your life.
- **Detailed analytics:** Get deep insights into your performance with charts and graphs.
- **Social features:** Share your progress with friends and join a supportive community.

**Cons:**
- **Can be overwhelming:** The sheer number of features can be a bit much for some users.
- **Subscription model:** Requires a subscription for the full range of features.
- **Learning curve:** It may take some time to learn how to use all of the app's features effectively.

### 7. Strides

**Best for:** The goal-oriented individual who wants to track more than just habits.

Strides is a versatile app that goes beyond simple habit tracking. It allows you to track four different types of goals: habits, targets, averages, and milestones. This makes it a powerful tool for tracking everything from your daily water intake to your progress on a major project. The app's dashboard gives you a clear overview of all your goals in one place.

Strides also offers a wealth of data and insights, with beautiful charts and graphs that help you visualize your progress over time. You can set daily, weekly, monthly, or even yearly goals, and the app will help you stay on track with reminders and progress reports. While it has a steeper learning curve than some of the other apps on this list, its flexibility and power are unmatched.

**Pros:**
- **Four tracker types:** Track habits, targets, averages, and milestones.
- **Powerful data visualization:** Get a clear overview of your progress with beautiful charts and graphs.
- **Flexible goal setting:** Set goals for any time frame, from daily to yearly.

**Cons:**
- **Steep learning curve:** It may take some time to learn how to use all of the app's features.
- **No social features:** Lacks the accountability of a social network.
- **Subscription model:** Requires a subscription for unlimited trackers.

### 8. Way of Life

**Best for:** The data-loving realist who appreciates flexibility and a forgiving approach to tracking.

Way of Life has been a popular choice for years, and its strength lies in its unique and flexible tracking system. Instead of a simple yes/no, you can mark habits as 'done,' 'not done,' or 'skipped.' This small distinction is a game-changer. It allows you to miss a day due to sickness or travel without breaking your streak, which fosters a more realistic and sustainable approach to habit formation.

The app's data visualization is another strong point. It presents your progress in a variety of charts and graphs, making it easy to spot trends and patterns in your behavior. You can also add notes to your entries, providing valuable context for your successes and failures. However, the lack of widgets and Apple Health integration are notable drawbacks.

**Pros:**
- **Flexible tracking:** The 'skip' option is a forgiving and realistic feature.
- **Excellent data visualization:** The charts and graphs make it easy to spot trends.
- **Note-taking feature:** Add context to your entries.

**Cons:**
- **No widgets:** Lacks the convenience of home screen or lock screen access.
- **No Apple Health integration:** You'll have to manually log your fitness and health data.
- **Reports of data loss:** Some users have reported losing their data after app updates.

### 9. Everyday

**Best for:** The visual motivator who gets a kick out of seeing their progress in a colorful, tangible way.

Everyday is all about the power of visualization. It displays your habits in a beautiful, color-coded grid that shows your progress over time. The more you complete your habits, the more colorful your grid becomes. It’s a simple but incredibly effective way to stay motivated and see how far you’ve come.

The app also has a unique “no-break-the-chain” feature that allows you to skip a day without breaking your streak, as long as you complete the habit the next day. This is a great way to stay motivated even when you have an off day. While the free version is limited to three habits, the premium version unlocks unlimited habits and a variety of color themes.

**Pros:**
- **Beautiful and motivating design:** The colorful grid is a powerful visual motivator.
- **Flexible streak tracking:** The “no-break-the-chain” feature is a forgiving and realistic approach.
- **Cross-platform sync:** Syncs across all your devices, including web, iOS, and Android.

**Cons:**
- **Limited free version:** You can only track three habits for free.
- **No Apple Health integration:** You’ll have to manually log your fitness and health data.
- **Limited features:** Lacks the advanced analytics and customization options of other apps.
